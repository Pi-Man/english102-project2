Path Planning Techniques
pick the right tool for the job

Situation 1

Environment

Unknown obstacles: The technique has no prior knowledge of any obstacles.

Optimizations

Optimize for least battery consumption: We want to use the least amount of battery power to get from the start to the goal.

Technique

Differential Evolution (DE) is the best choice for this situation. In a study done by Ad Wahab et al. it was shown that DE had the least battery consumption when navigating an unknown environment.

Situation 2

Environment

Complex and fully known and static obstacles: The technique is aware of all obstacles and their shapes/sizes which may be complex and of different sizes. Additionally all obstacles are fixed and can not change.

Optimizations

Shortest computation time: We want to spend the least amount of time possible computing the path.

Technique

Rapidly exploring Random Tree (RRT) works well for this situation. It is a quick technique and we don't care about path length or unknown/dynamic obstacles.

Situation 3

Environment

Complex mostly known static obstacles: The technique is aware of all larger obstacles with some inaccuracy. Additionally, there may be other small obstacles that the technique is unaware of initially.

Optimizations

Shortest path and obey kinematic constraints, secondary optimization of low calculation time: We want the shortest path we can get while also conforming to the kinematic constraints of the robot.

Technique

A* + Least Squares Policy Iteration (A* + LSPI) is a technique developed specifically for this situation. In a paper by Zuo, L. et al., this technique was compared to A* and RRT, and it is shown that this technique leads to a near optimal path that conforms to the kinematic constraints of the robot and does not increase computation time by a large amount.

Other Techniques

  • Genetic Algorithm (GA)
  • Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO)
  • Cuckoo Search Algorithm (CSA)
  • Dijkstra's Algorithm
  • Potential Field (PF)
  • Probabilistic Road Map (PRM)
  • Partition Based Path Planning (PBPP)
  • Logistics Beetle Algorithm Search - Pigeon Inspired Optimization (LBAS-PIO)
